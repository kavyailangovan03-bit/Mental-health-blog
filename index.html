Introduction

In the modern workplace, balance matters. This vlog explores how simple changes in work patterns — like flexible hours and remote days — can influence stress, happiness, and productivity. Using a compact dataset of 20 employees across departments, we visualize patterns and share concise, actionable inferences to help organizations design healthier, more effective workplaces.

Average Stress Level by Department

Inference

1) Sales and Marketing teams exhibit higher average stress levels.
2) HR and Design departments show relatively lower stress, suggesting balanced workloads.
3) These insights highlight the need for targeted stress management strategies.

Productivity vs Remote Work % (average trend)

Inference

1) Productivity rises steadily as remote work increases, stabilizing beyond 70%.
2) Excessive remote hours may reduce collaboration impact, leveling productivity.
3) Hybrid work balance appears most beneficial for sustained performance.

Conclusion

Small, data-driven changes in how we work can produce meaningful improvements in wellbeing and output. This vlog encourages teams to test hybrid policies, monitor stress levels, and prioritize interventions where they matter most.
